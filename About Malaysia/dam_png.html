PENANG


Penang named in Malay modern name Pulau Pinang, which means "areca nut island".
Penang is famous for the UNESCO World Heritage site at Georgetown, for its hawker food,
and for its pristine beaches among other things.

George Town is the colorful, multicultural capital of the Malaysian island of Penang.
Once an important Straits of Malacca trading hub, the city is known for its
British colonial buildings, Chinese shophouses and mosques.
