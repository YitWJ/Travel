SARAWAK


Sarawak, a Malaysian state on Borneo, stretches along the islandâ€™s northwest coast, including many beaches on the South China Sea.
It's known for the rugged, dense rainforest of its interior, much of it protected parkland.
The capital, Kuching, is a bustling city set on the Sarawak River.
Its 19th-century landmarks include the Astana, former palace of the White Rajahs, and Fort Margherita, built to thwart pirates.

Sarawak is the largest state in Malaysia, Sarawak is home to 27 ethnic groups, speaking 45 languages and dialects,
and each with their own stories, colorful cultures, traditions, and beliefs that makes Sarawak a cultural extravaganza just waiting to be explored.
